Child Safety Standards

Global Research Nexus has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation, including child sexual abuse material.

Zero tolerance. Child sexual abuse and exploitation are prohibited on Global Research Nexus. We remove prohibited content, take action against involved accounts and report to appropriate regional or national authorities as required by law.

1. Scope

These standards apply to all content, images, links, profiles and interactions available through the Global Research Nexus mobile app and services. The app is intended for adult research professionals, but these protections apply regardless of a user's stated age.

2. What we prohibit

Child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) includes content or behaviour that sexually abuses, exploits or endangers a person under 18. Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) includes visual depictions, including generated or altered imagery, involving a child in sexually explicit conduct.

We prohibit CSAM; grooming; sextortion; sexual solicitation involving a child; trafficking or facilitating child sexual exploitation; sexualised depictions of children; links or instructions that enable access to such material; and attempts to evade child-safety enforcement.

3. Report a child-safety concern

In the app, open the actions for the relevant post, choose Report, select Sexual content, Illegal content or Other, and state that it is a child-safety concern in the details. Reports are private.

Do not download, copy, forward or email suspected CSAM. Report the post or account without attaching additional copies. For concerns that cannot be reported in the app, contact nihalcastelino@gmail.com with the subject “Urgent Global Research Nexus child safety report” and provide only the account, post or link identifiers needed to locate the concern.

If a child is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or the appropriate law-enforcement authority. Global Research Nexus is not an emergency service.

4. Our response

Child-safety reports receive priority handling. Depending on the circumstances, we may promptly restrict access to reported material, remove content, disable posting, suspend or terminate accounts, prevent repeat registration and preserve limited records where required for safety, investigation or legal compliance.

When we obtain actual knowledge of apparent CSAM or other reportable child exploitation, we act in accordance with applicable law and report to the appropriate regional or national authorities. We cooperate with valid legal requests and preserve relevant records only as permitted or required by law.

5. Privacy and evidence handling

Access to child-safety reports and preserved records is restricted to authorised personnel and service providers with a legitimate need. Reporters should not conduct their own investigation or create additional copies of suspected material. Information is retained only for as long as necessary for safety, legal compliance and enforcement.

6. Enforcement

Violations may result in immediate content removal and permanent account termination without prior warning. Attempts to conceal, redistribute or facilitate access to prohibited material may result in additional restrictions and reports to authorities. Account holders may appeal an account action, but illegal material will not be restored.
